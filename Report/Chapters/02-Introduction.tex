\chapter[Introduction]{Introduction}
\label{cp:introduction}

In this project, we are going to use the software radio technique to simulate
the hardware problems and try to make it as close as it is with the antenna,
while also using the techniques we learned through our communications course.
We are going to design the transmitter first. Our code at the transmitter will
define the modulation technique and the coding used. Once the digital data is
generated, it goes through a Digital-to-Analog Converter (DAC) so it can
actually take the form of three different transmitted signals: Polar RZ, Polar
NRZ, and Unipolar. However, the bit-stream generated at the transmitter is
actually random, and there can also be asynchronization between the transmitter
and the receiver, which is also random. Therefore, in our simulation, we will
generate a random bit-stream, and the output will be treated as a random
process. Then, for each generated ensemble, we will calculate its statistical
mean, the time mean, the ensemble and time autocorrelation functions, and the
bandwidth. We will also determine whether the random process is stationary and
ergodic.
